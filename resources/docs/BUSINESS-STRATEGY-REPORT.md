- **Accept multiple payment methods.** Square for in-person, Stripe for online booking deposits.

### 5.2 Review Generation Strategy

Reviews are critical for a new local business. They directly impact Google search rankings and build trust with potential clients.

#### Goal: 15-20 Google Reviews within the first 3 months

#### Tactics:

1. **Post-Session Follow-Up Email** (Automated)
   - Send 24 hours after each session
   - Include a thank-you message, any homework/aftercare reminders
   - Include a direct link to leave a Google Review
   - Keep the ask simple: "If you found value in our session, a brief Google review helps others find their path to healing."

2. **Physical Reminder Card**
   - Hand a small card with QR code linking to Google Review page after each in-person session
   - Include with herbal tea packaging

3. **Incentive (Ethical)**
   - Offer a small sample herbal tea blend or $10 off next session for clients who leave an honest review
   - Important: Never ask for only positive reviews — ask for honest ones

4. **Response Protocol**
   - Respond to every review within 48 hours
   - Thank positive reviewers specifically by referencing something personal
   - Address negative reviews professionally and compassionately (rare for this industry, but preparation matters)

5. **Multi-Platform Approach**
   - Primary: Google Reviews (impacts search rankings most)
   - Secondary: Yelp (strong in Reno wellness market)
   - Tertiary: Facebook Reviews

## 7. Email Marketing Campaign Timeline

### 7.1 Platform Selection: MailerLite

**Selected Platform: MailerLite** — Free plan up to 1,000 subscribers

#### Why MailerLite over Mailchimp

| Factor | MailerLite (Free Plan) | Mailchimp (Free Plan) |
|--------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Subscriber limit** | 1,000 subscribers | 500 subscribers |
| **Monthly email sends** | 12,000 emails/mo | 1,000 emails/mo (hard cap) |
| **Automation** | Full automation included on free plan (welcome sequences, triggers, workflows) | Automations severely limited — only single-step "customer journeys" on free |
| **Landing pages** | Unlimited landing pages included free | 1 landing page on free plan |
| **Email templates** | Drag-and-drop editor with modern templates | Drag-and-drop, but many templates locked behind paid tiers |
| **Signup forms / popups** | Embedded forms, popups, and promotional banners all included free | Embedded forms included, popups limited |
| **A/B testing** | Available on free plan | Not available on free plan |
| **Reporting** | Click maps, opens by location, device reports | Basic open/click reporting |
| **Branding** | Small "Sent with MailerLite" footer on free | Mailchimp badge in footer on free |
| **Paid upgrade** | $10/mo for 500 subs, $15/mo for 1,000 subs (removes branding, adds advanced features) | $13/mo for 500 subs, $20/mo for 1,500 subs |
| **Ease of use** | Clean, intuitive interface. Less clutter. | More features but cluttered dashboard, steeper learning curve |
| **Integrations** | Integrates with Acuity Scheduling, Stripe, Shopify, WordPress, Zapier | Similar integrations but some require paid plan |

**Bottom Line:** For a solo practitioner starting from zero subscribers, MailerLite gives significantly more for free. The two biggest wins are:

1. **2x the subscriber limit** (1,000 vs. 500) — Bree won't hit a paywall as quickly
2. **Full automation on free** — The welcome email sequence (4 emails over 2 weeks) that converts prospects to clients works out of the box on MailerLite's free plan. On Mailchimp's free plan, this would require a paid upgrade.

MailerLite also integrates directly with Acuity Scheduling, so when a client books, they can be automatically added to the email list and tagged by service type.

**MailerLite Setup Steps:**
1. Create account at mailerlite.com
